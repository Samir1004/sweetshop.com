ABOUT US

The word “mishti” has a special significance in the Bengali culinary lexicon. Sweets shop means a heartful shop for Bengalis. It refers to not only the taste of sweetness, but also a plethora of desserts that are not just a part of a meal, but also eaten on their own, served to guests, and offered to the gods. Mishti, therefore, is a rough title given to a plethora of delicacies of the sweet nature and can be divided into distinct parts not just by the way it is made, but also through the ingredients used. In the former aspect, mishti can be divided into parts based on the making technique, like fried, boiled, soaked in sweet syrup, formed into individual pieces, or sold by the gram. In the latter, mishti can be divided into the ingredients it is made out of like “chhana” (cottage cheese), “kheer”(khoya), a variety of lentils and flours, coconut, and rice.
